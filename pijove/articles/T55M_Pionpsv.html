T-55M Pionpsv, Finnish Armoured Recon that appeared when ‘Double Nation Pack: REDS DLC ‘ was released.

Stats

T-55M Pionpsv Stats card (Fig.)

  • Cost: 65pts, 15pts more expensive than T-55M for armour, optic and stealth improvements
  • Size: Medium, is good
  • Armour: 10/4/2/2, Heavily armoured for a recon unit, and can endure artillery.
  • Optics: Very good, Decent trade off between optic quality and card availability
  • Speed: 50km/h, slow but typical of a T-55
  • Autonomy: 600km, good
  • Stealth: Medium, standard value on ‘stealth’ vehicles
  • Prototype: No

Main Cannon (D-10T)

  • Range: 2275m, maximum a tank can have
  • Accuracy: 60% is good
  • Stabiliser: 15% is terrible
  • AP: 17, is very good for recon, Side shots will harm heavily armoured enemies.
  • HE: 3, Standard (Typical T-55)
  • Rate of Fire: 8, is ok, (Typical T-55)

Comparison

The T-55M is similar to the original non-DLC stealth tank the chinese ZTQ-62G, the dutch Leopard 1, is also a similar and formidable adversary (Fig.). When compared against T-55 in the tank tab, the unit still hold up well. Optics and stealth are an added cost (about 15pts?) which seems like good value in my opinon.

Recon

Two main factors make ‘Stealth’ tanks good:

  1. Optics; so they can actually spot targets especially those hiding in cover.
  2. Stealth; making the unit hard to detect. If the enemy has no recon unit to spot it they have to be in very close proximity to do so.

Note when you fire, the stealth decreases. Main cannons reveal more stealth that auto-cannons for example.

With ‘Very Good’ Optics you can spot tanks (‘poor’ stealth) at 3500m in the open or 2150m if it is hiding in a hedgeline. Recon vehicles (‘medium’ stealth) can be spotted 3500m in the open or 1450m if it is hiding in headline. Note 1450m is in range of all tank cannons and some autocannons, so you can be ambushed by the enemy. Standard Infantry (‘good’ stealth) can be spotted 3000m in the open, 1075m in a hedgeline which is slightly out of RPG range (875m), urban sectors require you to be within 750m. Recon Infantry and 2-man teams like ATGM infantry (‘very good’ stealth) can be spotted at 2350m, ATGM missile range can be greater than this. When in cover to detect this units you need to be closer than 875m, which is the same distance at a long ranged RPG. 2 man Sniper Recon (‘Exceptional’ stealth) are very hard to detect. 1950m in the open, 725m in cover, and 525m in an urban sector. Tactics

Card availability

For recon vehicle, the quality of the optic effects the number available per card. Typical numbers are: 12x Good, 6x Very Good, 4x Exceptional. Very Good, is balanced between the 2 extremes.

Defensive / Offensive, Stance

Same principles apply to whether attacking of defending. The main difference, to attack you will need supporting units to push land. Example having an infantry unit can draw fire from tanks or infantry hiding in cover, exposing themselves to be countered by the T-55M or not to stumble into an ambush. While in defence it is possible to get away operating solo if an enemy approaches you. Find cover, this will significantly boost the stealth value. You biggest threat is rival recon, this should be a priority target. When is eliminated, you’ll be able to shoot at the enemy uncontested if far way enough. Secondary target should be enemies which are getting extremely close to you, if they spot you they will light you up other enemies to target you. Context decides whether you should turn off the main gun or not. If stealth is removed when shooting, and the target(s) can return significant damage. The gun should remain off until the situation changes. Be aware although you may never get spotted ‘in game’ the enemy may use artillery on the location they suspect the recon unit.

Counter Recon

When it comes to recon wars, you’ll need artillery to support, either to provide smoke or to cause morale damage. This is where it becomes chess like. Note a recon infantry will spot a recon vehicle at a greater range (1450 vs 875m). If the unit move out of cover it will get instantly spotted. 1450m will increase to 3500m/2350m. ‘Stealth Tanks’ can be annoying if you ignore them. So you need options on how to cope.

  • Use recon infantry, wading through cover getting closer until they are revealed.
  • Smoke the location of unit so it cant ambush you.
  • Stun location of unit so it is an unfair fight in your favour.
  • Use a unit, to draw fire from it, so it can be revealed and countered.

Field Research

So I had a good game which I think managed to showcase this unit. It was spawned at the start of the game and survived to the end. The area it was in wasn’t my priority until later game, which explains why it was solo for so long.

Enemy initially spammed M1 tanks (a), since they had no recon, I was able to fire uncontested, whilst providing recon for teammates. Later with teamwork we pushed, I did my best to cover his recon infantry, which was great as it meant we had much better recon information with less risk (b). I like having passive recon in front, having a stealth tank just behind made it harder for them to dislodge. Note, I was very cautious about being ambushed so I made sure my infantry cleared the neighbouring forest first (b). Enemy were trying to set up Ambushes (c), this time the tried to unload the ATGM too close. Later on an ATGM did hit the tank which damaged it heavily but luckily it wasn’t destroyed.

The lack of recon by the enemy (not protecting their own recon), is what allowed this tank to cause so much devastation, including BAZ, ADATS, and many tanks worth more than the units value (Fig.). When a unit is not spotted, the enemy is more prone to showing the vulnerable side armour.

Summary

Ignoring the Meme-84AN, the T-55M Pionpsv is a top tier Recon tank, More armoured and higher AP than it’s similar rival the Dutch Leopard tank. It is a very good unit. The terrible stabiliser means it should fire when static. Understanding recon mechanics is key for optimal performance of this unit. It’s is significantly better than other nations versions of the Recon T-55. Even in comparison to other T-55 variants in the tank tab, it is still very good. Viable option for deck selection.
