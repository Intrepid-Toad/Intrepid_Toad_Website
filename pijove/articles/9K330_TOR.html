Introduction

9K330 TOR / Fla-Kom TOR, both stats clones, except the Fla-Kom TOR is prototyped.

Stats

“The project was given strict design specifications to meet; Tor had to provide extended detection and tracking of fast, low radar cross section targets and be capable of quickly and efficiently dealing with massed air raids, whilst providing a high degree of automation and integration with other air defence assets.In order to meet these demanding specifications, the designers used a variety of new technologies, including advanced Passive electronically scanned array radar for improved detection and tracking performance, enhanced digital information processing, and vertically launched missiles to improve reaction time and increase the number of readily available munitions. After a period of testing and evaluation between December 1983 and December 1984, the land based system was accepted into service on March 19, 1986”- wiki history

Stats (Fig.)

  • Accuracy: 60%, is good but note planes may have ECM which modify accuracy
  • HE: 7, good, two shots will take out planes or strong helicopters.
  • Range- Helicopter: 3150m is great, as it is beyond distance of helicopter AT missiles. Note it is longest range anti-helo AA Eastern Bloc can get.
  • Range- Airplanes: 3850m, medium ranged. Usual distance is 3500m-4550m
  • Reload: 30s, not an issue considering all 8 missiles are ready to fire
  • Speed: 65km/h, Fast for tracked AA
  • Autonomy: 450km, is ok but a bit low.
  • Radar [RAD]: “This weapons uses radar guidance, making it vulnerable to anti-radar missiles. Turning the weapon off will avoid this threat”

Anti-Helicopter

A single TOR can devastate a blob of cobras. (Fig.) 5 missiles were launched and 5 hit within 14s. The combination of; launching missiles quickly and the large splash damage make it effective at this. It only takes a single missile to destroy a Cobra helicopter.

Anti-Plane

With 8 missiles ready to fire, if the enemy spams planes, the TOR can cope. In-game a couple of TOR were slightly behind some allied units, 3 F-4’s flew into the air space(Fig.). 6 missiles were launched in quick succession, all 3 planes were destroyed by the pair of TORs. Planes may of been hit by some IR AA in the front lines, requiring only 1 missile to destroy.

AA Comparison

For context of usual AA platforms available. Eastern bloc have OSA, TOR & units that have same missiles stats as the BUK (Fig.).

SEAD- Suppression of Enemy Air Defences

“This anti-radiation missile will lock on enemy radar and be guided on them as long as they remain active”- SEAD tag SEAD Planes specialisation is to locate and destroy RADAR equipped units. SEAD planes usually have high ECM and have stealth too. Note the three different ranges of SEAD missiles (Fig.) and they all outrange the TOR.

  • SEAD Planes can locate RADAR that is turned on.
  • SEAD Planes can strike RADAR that is turned on. If your RADAR is turned OFF, SEAD can’t harm or give away your location.

Usage

Be aware you are a high value target. Artillery will attempt to destroy you, so move after firing, as it is easy to locate the origin of AA missile trails. Since artillery will shoot at your position, don’t have the AA beside units. By default I have my RADAR turned OFF. IF a plane flies into range THEN I turn ON my RADAR, WHEN done shooting, I turn the RADAR OFF, and relocate to different position. You can reduce the ‘micro intensity’ by binding the units to a group. I have my RADAR AA bound to a key. (CTRL + [NUM]) The shortcut key to turn off weapons is ‘H’.

If you manage you RADAR AA well you’ll frequently see SEAD attempting to seek you out, they may fly too deep into airspace in an attempt to locate and destroy you. Since SEAD can only launch a missiles when it is facing you, it is safe to turn on RADAR when it is facing away. (Fig.) The two main ways ‘Air’ players will try to destroy your RADAR AA with planes are;

  • SEAD plane flying behind the strike plane, baiting you to turning on your radar.
  • SEAD plane to keep your RADAR turned off and bomb the location.

An important point to note is, when a SEAD missile has been launched and you turn off the radar, the SEAD missile will target the last location of where the RADAR was turned on. So just turning off the RADAR is not enough, to evade the missile you need to move (Fig.). I find it works best when your unit is already moving and toggle the RADAR ON/OFF quickly. I do this regularly to Ka-52. Missiles from planes seem to travel faster though.

Microing AA can be intense and frantic at times, particularly if it is a combined air assault (Fig.) Helicopters trying to rush you, SEAD trying to suppress you, bombers and artillery trying to blow up your location.

Myth busting: “Can RADAR shoot through smoke?”

An Experiment vs helicopters concluded that Line of Sight is still needed (Fig.). The TOR are unable to target any of the helicopters. Haven’t tested on planes, but predict the same, just it may be harder to smoke AA unless they are engulfed in the smoke.

Conclusion

Balanced rapid firing RADAR AA unit. Sacrifices some Anti-Plane range for increased Anti-Helicopter range adding to versatility. Good off-road speed means it is possible to relocate to avoid artillery.
